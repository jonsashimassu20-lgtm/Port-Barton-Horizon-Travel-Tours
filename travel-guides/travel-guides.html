How to Get to Port Barton: The Ultimate Travel Guide

PORT BARTON is a peaceful, laid-back paradise hidden in the town of San Vicente, Palawan. Unlike other commercialized destinations, getting here requires a bit of planning. Whether you are landing in Puerto Princesa or El Nido, here is the easiest way to reach this coastal gem.

From Puerto Princesa (Most Common Route)

Most travelers enter Port Barton via the Puerto Princesa International Airport (PPS).

  • Shared or Private Van (Highly Recommended): The fastest and most comfortable way. The travel time is around 3 to 4 hours via smooth, winding scenic roads.

  • By Public Bus: Available at the Bgy. Irawan Bus Terminal, but takes 5 to 6 hours due to multiple stops

How to get to Taytay?

Getting to Taytay is easy since it sits directly along Palawan's main highway. The best option is an air-conditioned van transfer, taking 3 to 4 hours from Puerto Princesa and 1.5 to 2 hours from El Nido. Public buses are also available at local terminals but take longer. Once there, you can easily explore Taytay’s famous Spanish fortress and islands.

How to get to Coron?

Getting to Coron is easy as it is a major travel hub in northern Palawan. The fastest way is via a direct flight to Busuanga Airport (USU) from Manila or Cebu, followed by a 30-minute van transfer to Coron Town. Alternatively, if you are coming from El Nido, you can take a 3.5-hour high-speed ferry across the sea.

How to get to Balabac?

Getting to Balabac requires more planning since it is located at the southernmost tip of Palawan. From Puerto Princesa, you need to take a 4 to 5-hour van transfer to Rio Tuba in Bataraza. From Rio Tuba, you will take a 3-hour public passenger boat to Balabac Island. Because boat schedules depend heavily on the weather, booking a private tour package is highly recommended for a safe and hassle-free trip.
